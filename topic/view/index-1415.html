What is cholera?

Cholera is an infectious disease that causes severe watery diarrhoea, which can lead to dehydration and even death if untreated.

It is caused by eating food or drinking water contaminated with bacteria.

It is a waterborne disease.

Cholera is a waterborne disease

 

Cholera is spread by drinking contaminated water

What causes cholera?

Contaminated water 

 

Cholera Bacteria 

Signs and symptoms of cholera

  • Severe rice-like diarrhoea

  • Violent vomiting

 

  • Dehydration – lose of body water

How can we prevent the spread of cholera? 

  • Washing our hands after visiting the toilet

  • Boil or treat drinking water

  • Treat water before drinking

 

  • Education people on the importance of proper hygiene

 

  • Cover food

  • Wash foods eaten raw eg. fruits

 

  • Keep toilets clean and covered

  • Seek medical attention immediately
