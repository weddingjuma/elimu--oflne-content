Conserving Energy

Methods of conserving energy

Conserving energy means protecting, preserving and managing energy resources well.

We have already learnt about the types of energy and their sources.

If the sources of energy are destroyed, or if we do not use the energy wisely, we may not have enough energy for use in the future. We therefore need to conserve energy.

Some of the methods of conserving energy include:

  • Using energy efficient devices.
  • Using energy sparingly eg switch lights off when not in use.
  • Using renewable sources of energy eg solar power
  • Planting trees.

a) Using energy efficient devices

Most people in Kenya use jikos and cooking stones when cooking.

Jikos burn charcoal or firewood as a source of energy.

Charcoal is made from wood, which is a renewable source of energy.

If more trees are felled to produce charcoal and few trees are grown, we will run out of trees which are an important source of energy in our country.

Use of energy efficient devices will help reduce the amount of wood fuel consumed in a particular period of time.

An improved jiko is an example of energy efficient device.

b) Using energy sparingly

Using energy sparingly means using it carefully to avoid unnecessary loss.

Energy is very important to us, and we have to use it sparingly so that we have enough in the future.

We can use energy sparingly by doing the following:

 

  • Taking advantage of the daylight.
  • You do not need to switch on the lights when there is a lot of light during the day.
  • It is not necessary to warm water for showering during a warm day.
  • While cooking, it is important to cover the food so that it cooks fast.

  • Use just the right amount of fuel while cooking.
  • This can be achieved by putting off fire immediately your food gets cooked.
  • During cold weather you could wear a sweater or warm clothing instead of lighting the fireplace or the jiko to warm yourself.
  • Maintaining vehicles well to reduce fuel consumption.

c) Using renewable energy

Sources of energy that cannot be exhausted are called renewable sources of energy.

Those that can be exhausted are termed non-renewable sources.

Examples of renewable sources of energy include:

  • solar energy – using heat energy from the sun.
  • biogas – energy from gas made using cow dung and other animal wastes.
  • geothermal energy – using heat from the interior of the earth to produce energy.
  • hydro – using energy from water.
  • wind energy – using the energy from moving air. 

 

Wind energy

Wind refers to moving air.

Wind has energy.

The energy from wind can be used to turn turbines in windmills, which convert the wind energy into electricity. 

Advantages of windmills

  1. The energy source which is wind is free.
  2. The source is renewable and will never run out.
  3. It is environment friendly.

Disadvantage of windmills

  1. Wind turbines are expensive. Installation of wind turbines is also expensive.
  2. Requires expertise to handle. 

Biogas

To make biogas you need a digester.

Place two amounts of water to one of cow dung in the digester.

As the dung ferments, biogas is produced.

The biogas rises to the top of the small metal drum.

A control tap directs the gas to either cooking or lighting area.

The remaining dung can be removed and used as manure.

Solar energy (energy from the sun)

The sun is the main source of energy.

Energy from the sun is also known as solar energy.

Solar energy is used in the following ways:

  • All crops and plants depend on energy from the sun to manufacture food during photosynthesis.

Manufactured food in plants is transferred to animals when they eat plants.

  • The sun is a source of warmth and light.

Solar panels can be used to trap solar energy.

The energy can then be used to heat water or to light houses.

Heat energy from the sun can also be used to dry harvested crops.

  • Heat energy from the sun causes evaporation in the water-cycle which eventually provides us with rain.

Advantages of solar energy

  1. It is free.
  2. It can be converted to electric energy.
  3. It is environmental friendly. 

d) Planting trees

Trees are important to the environment because they :

  • are a source of fuel to most families.
  • form part of the water cycle
  • purify air by producing oxygen.
  • control soil erosion.
  • are a source of some medicines.
  • absorb excess carbon dioxide from the atmosphere during photosynthesis.

The government of Kenya advises us that every time we cut down one tree, we should plant two.

This is aimed at restoring the forest cover. At the moment, however, the rate of cutting down trees is higher than the rate of planting and growing them.

This is why our forest cover is declining. Scientists believe that forests should cover at least 10% of the total area of a country.

Our forest cover however, is only 1.7%, and this may be the reason why our rivers and lakes are drying up.

To help conserve our forests, we can

  • use other sources of fuel, like cow dung, sawdust, the sun, coffee husks and wind among others.
  • reduce the misuse of charcoal by using energy conserving jikos.
  • practice afforestation or reforestation. 

Afforestation is the planting of new trees to create forests. Reforestation is the planting of trees to replace those that have been cut.
