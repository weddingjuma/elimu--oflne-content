Loss In Buying And Selling  by  Alice Akinyi

6

Terms

  • Buying -  is to acquire or obtain goods/ property for a price.
  • Selling - is handing over goods or an item in  exchange for money.
  • Buying price (BP) - is the price at which a trader buys an item.
  • Selling price - is the price at which a tradwer sells an item.
  • Loss - occurs when atrader sells an item at a price lower thanthe price at which he/she bought the item.
  • It also means the shortage amount incurred on buying price  

Example 1

1.Ochola bought a cow at sh. 25,000 and sold it later at sh. 23000.  

How much was the loss?

Solution

Loss = buying price(Bp) - selling price(Sp)
           Bp = sh.25000
           Sp = sh.23000
           25000 - 23000
           = sh.2000

Example 2

2.Ole maasai bought two goats for sh.8000. He sold them later at sh.3800 each. How much was the loss?

Sh.3800

Sh.3800

Solution:

Loss = Bp- Sp
Bp = sh.8000
Sp = 2 goats x sh. 3800
        Sh. 7600
therefore the loss will be
Sh. 8000-7600 = sh. 400
