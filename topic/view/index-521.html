Classification of plants

Plants can be classified into two groups:

  1.  Green plants  and fungus

  2. Flowering  and  non-flowering plants

Fungus

They lack chlorophyll and are therefore not able to manufacture their own food by photosynthesis.

They instead, take-in their nutrients from dead and decaying animal or plant materials.

Examples of such plants are bread mould, mushrooms, toad stool and puffball.

Flowering or non-flowering plants

Flowering plants:

Plants that produce flowers are called flowering plants.

It is through the flowers that seeds and fruits are formed.

A plant can then germinate from the seeds or fruits.

Examples of flowering plants are maize, oranges, beans, bougainvillea and hibiscus.

Non-flowering plants

Plants that do not produce flowers are called non-flowering plants.

These plants cannot reproduce by producing seed.

Instead, the non-flowering plants reproduce through production of tiny structures called spores.

Examples of non-flowering plants are bread mould, mushrooms, algae (simple plants which make pond water green) fern and lichens.

Comparing wind pollinated flowers with insect pollinated flowers

Wind pollinated flowers  Insect pollinated flowers
Have small dull flowers Have large and brightly coloured flowers
Have no nectar and no scent  Produce nectar and often have strong scent
The stigma is on a long stigma stalk  Have a small but firm stigma so that it is exposed to the wind
Produce small and smooth pollen Produce large sticky pollen
Produce pollen grains  Produce few pollen
